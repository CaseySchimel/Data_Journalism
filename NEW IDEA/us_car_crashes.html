US Car Crash Death Rate Over the Years

Trends in US Car Crash deaths

The data shows a significant upward trend in car crash deaths over the years. Here are some key observations:

  • Early 1900s: The number of car crashes was relatively low due to the lower number of vehicles.
  • Mid-20th Century: A significant increase in car crashes was observed as car ownership became more common.
  • Recent Years: Improved car safety and regulations have led to a decrease in fatalities per 100 million VMT.

Key Factors Contributing to Car Crashes

  • Driver Behavior: Speeding, distracted driving, and driving under the influence.
  • Vehicle Factors: Mechanical failures, tire blowouts, and other vehicular issues.
  • Environmental Factors: Weather conditions, road conditions, and lighting.

Something to note:

  • I've included on my graph the average United States Population by year since 1950 (around when they truely began keeping records).
